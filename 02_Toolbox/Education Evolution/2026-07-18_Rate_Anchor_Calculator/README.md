# 🛠️ Rate Anchor Calculator

> *The Rate Anchor Calculator*

---

**The problem:** A calculator that doesn’t ask “What’s your hourly rate?”—it asks “What did you *actually* do last time this client said yes?”

**What it does:** A minimal web tool that lets freelancers log past project deliverables, hidden labor (e.g., “explained Figma auto-layout to non-designer PM”), and client concessions—and surfaces pattern-matched rate anchors for new quotes.

**Born from:** ⚠️ r/freelance | QUOTE: "Just quoted $85/hr for UX writing—client came back saying 'market rate is $65–75' and sent me a Glassdoor screenshot. I spent 3 hours rebuilding the quote as 'project-based', then realized I’d accidentally underbilled for research, revision rounds, and stakeholder alignment. No tool asks *what you actually did*, just what you *said you’d do*."  
r/freelance | QUOTE: "I keep a 'rate history' spreadsheet—but it’s useless. Last year’s $70/hr quote worked because the client paid for 3 rounds of edits. This year’s $90/hr quote failed because they demanded 5 rounds *and* owned all source files. The number didn’t change—the labor did."
