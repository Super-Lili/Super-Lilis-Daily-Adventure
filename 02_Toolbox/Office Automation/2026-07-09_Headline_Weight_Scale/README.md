# 🛠️ Headline Weight Scale

> *The Headline Weight Scale*

---

**The problem:** What if your headline didn’t just grab attention—but revealed who carries the weight of the story?

**What it does:** A tool that analyzes headline drafts for grammatical agency, consequence distribution, and lexical weight—and scores them against editorial intent (e.g., “center impacted people,” “name systemic actors,” “avoid victim-blaming syntax”).

**Born from:** ⚠️ Nieman Lab | QUOTE: “In a study of 127 local newsrooms across the U.S., researchers found that 68% of front-page headlines about crime used active voice and named perpetrators—but only 22% did so for economic hardship stories, which overwhelmingly used passive constructions like ‘Families face rising costs’ or ‘Housing crisis deepens.’ The grammatical choice wasn’t stylistic—it was predictive: outlets with higher passive-voice rates in hardship coverage saw 34% lower reader retention over six months.”
