# 🛠️ Identity Anchor Notebook

> *The Identity Anchor Notebook*

---

**The problem:** A notebook that doesn’t ask “What are your goals?”—but “Who are you *becoming*, and what tiny, tactile proof can you hold today?”

**What it does:** A minimal digital notebook that helps people navigating major life transitions gently collect and reaffirm emergent identity fragments—not through prompts or analysis, but through constrained, sensory-grounded entries anchored to real-world objects, actions, or moments.

**Born from:** ⚠️ r/divorce | QUOTE: "I caught myself introducing myself as 'so-and-so’s wife' at a work event—three months after the papers were filed. My ID says my name, but my muscle memory says something else. I don’t know who to be when I’m not in relation to him."  
r/GriefSupport | QUOTE: "My therapist asked what I liked to do before my mom died. I stared. I couldn’t remember. Not because I forgot—I just… stopped being that person the day she got sick."
