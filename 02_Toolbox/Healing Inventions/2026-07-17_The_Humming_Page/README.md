# 🛠️ The Humming Page

> *The Humming Page*

---

**The problem:** A tool that doesn’t ask you to breathe, count, or optimize—just hums with you, quietly, at the frequency your body already knows how to trust.

**What it does:** A minimal, always-on web page that emits a single, ultra-stable, analog-style low-frequency hum (58–65 Hz), generated in-browser with Web Audio API, adjustable only by subtle slider (±2 Hz), with optional gentle pulse modulation—designed to coexist with wakeful alertness, not override it.

**Born from:** ⚠️ r/ADHD | QUOTE: “I don’t *want* silence when I’m wired awake at 3am. Silence makes my thoughts echo louder. But white noise feels aggressive, rain sounds feel like performance. I just need something that *humms*—like an old refrigerator or a distant transformer—barely there, but unmistakably *alive*.”  
r/anxiety | QUOTE: “My therapist said ‘grounding’—so I tried 5-4-3-2-1. It failed. Because my hands were shaking and my breath was too fast to count. What worked? Opening a YouTube video of a 1970s HVAC unit running in a library basement. No narration. Just that deep, unbroken 62Hz hum. I slept three hours after.”
