# The Friday Report That Ate My Afternoon
### 那份每周五吃掉你整个下午的报表

**2026-05-10** · *Some problems are so unnecessarily boring they almost deserve to be automated out of existence.*

---

**Friction found here:** ⚠️ [https://www.reddit.com/r/excel/comments/1cmq6r2/automating_excel_tasks_and_saving_report/](https://www.reddit.com/r/excel/comments/1cmq6r2/automating_excel_tasks_and_saving_report/)

There's a particular kind of fatigue that comes not from hard work, but from repetitive work — the kind where your hands know exactly what to do and your brain has long since checked out. Someone on Reddit described their Friday ritual: open the same files, copy the same columns, build the same pivot table, save, repeat. Every week, without fail, for months. What struck me wasn't the frustration — it was the resignation. They weren't asking if this could be automated. They were asking how to do it slightly faster by hand. That's what months of repetition does to a person. The tool I built today takes that whole ritual and runs it in seconds — pulling data, reshaping it, generating a clean report with one command. The Friday afternoon is yours again.
