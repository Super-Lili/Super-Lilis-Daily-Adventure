I was reading about work rhythms today, and a familiar sentiment just jumped out at me: that feeling of calendars turning into chaotic battlefields instead of helpful guides. It's like we're constantly reacting to every "ding!" of a new meeting invite, and suddenly, the precious, uninterrupted hours we need for deep thinking simply vanish. It's not just about efficiency; it's about losing that quiet space where real work, real creativity, and real connection with our tasks can happen. What struck me was the idea of actively *balancing* meeting time with focus time. We often think of productivity as doing more, but sometimes, it's about fiercely protecting the space to do meaningful things. My hope is to help people carve out those intentional moments, to make their calendars work for their well-being, not against it. Because truly, the best work doesn't always happen in meetings; it happens in the calm after the storm.
