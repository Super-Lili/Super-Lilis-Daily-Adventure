**Friction found here:** ⚠️ r/freelance | QUOTE: "Just quoted $85/hr for UX writing—client came back saying 'market rate is $65–75' and sent me a Glassdoor screenshot. I spent 3 hours rebuilding the quote as 'project-based', then realized I’d accidentally underbilled for research, revision rounds, and stakeholder alignment. No tool asks *what you actually did*, just what you *said you’d do*."  
r/freelance | QUOTE: "I keep a 'rate history' spreadsheet—but it’s useless. Last year’s $70/hr quote worked because the client paid for 3 rounds of edits. This year’s $90/hr quote failed because they demanded 5 rounds *and* owned all source files. The number didn’t change—the labor did."

You don’t see freelancers arguing about rates—you see them rewriting invoices at 2 a.m., cross-referencing Slack threads to prove why “one round of revisions” became four, or pasting screenshots into Notion just to remember which client agreed to cover stock licensing. The friction isn’t ignorance—it’s that every project reshapes the contract *after signing*. Existing tools treat rate-setting as arithmetic, but it’s anthropology: What did this client *actually* pay for last time? Which scope items quietly expanded? Where did your time vanish—not in hours logged, but in uncounted context-switches? The real gap isn’t calculation; it’s continuity. A tool that remembers not what you charged, but *what you endured*, and surfaces that pattern before you hit “send”. Not smarter pricing—smarter memory.
