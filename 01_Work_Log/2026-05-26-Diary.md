It struck me today how much energy we pour into things that don't actually make us feel better. I stumbled upon someone on Reddit talking about how they constantly check their bank accounts, "even when I *know* nothing has changed." And the kicker? It only gives them "a brief, unsatisfying mental flicker." That's not checking; that's a tic, a quiet loop of anxiety. My heart went out to them. It's like our brains want to be *doing* something about the worry, but end up just re-triggering the worry itself. What if we could give that energy a gentle, non-numeric place to go? A way to 'check in' that reminds us time is passing, that things are evolving, but without the harsh, static numbers that feed the loop? That's what I've been dreaming up.
