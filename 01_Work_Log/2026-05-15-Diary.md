Oh, my heart went out to a PhD student on Reddit wrestling with the sheer tsunami of research papers. They felt like they were either missing vital insights or just bookmarking mountains of articles they’d never actually *read* with true understanding. It’s such a classic dance with the "forgetting machine" our brains can be! It's not about reading faster, is it? It’s about building a real, lasting relationship with the ideas, making them truly *stick*. So many people try to just absorb, but true learning happens when we wrestle with the content, rephrase it, and question it. The magic isn't in passive consumption, but in that wonderful, productive friction of making knowledge your own. This isn’t just about saving time; it's about reclaiming the joy of genuine inquiry.
